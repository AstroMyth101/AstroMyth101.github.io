sections:
  - eyebrow: Brief
    heading: "A voltage is not a measurement until you calibrate it."
    body: |
      An ultrasonic sensor returns an analog voltage, not a distance. The task was to turn
      that raw signal into a **trustworthy displacement reading** — and to document how, so the
      result is reproducible rather than a number pulled off a multimeter.
  - eyebrow: Calibration
    heading: "Mapping 0–3.3 V to real displacement."
    body: |
      Acquiring the sensor's output across its **0–3.3 V** range and pairing each reading with
      a known displacement gives a calibration relationship that converts future voltages into
      distance. Working within a fixed input range also frames the practical limits:
      resolution, and where the sensor stops being linear.
  - eyebrow: Documentation
    heading: "The analysis is the deliverable."
    body: |
      The acquisition setup, the raw data, and the conversion analysis were written up so the
      measurement chain — sensor → voltage → calibrated displacement — is transparent and
      repeatable. In instrumentation, an honest, documented method is worth more than a
      confident-looking number.
  - eyebrow: Value
    heading: "Measurement fundamentals, done properly."
    body: |
      Signal conditioning, calibration, and clear reporting are the unglamorous core of
      instrumentation — the difference between *reading* a sensor and actually *measuring*
      something with it.
